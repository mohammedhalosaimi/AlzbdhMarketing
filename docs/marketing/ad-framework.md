# الزبدة (alzbdah) — Ad & Brand Framework
### AI Travel Concierge for Saudi & GCC Travelers

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Aligned with:** PRD v6.1, BRD v2.0

---

## TL;DR

- **Position:** "Your trip, thought through" — calm AI concierge, not a booking engine
- **Formula:** Every ad follows 3 steps: show the friction → invite gently → show the outcome
- **Visual style:** 70% whitespace, minimalist, sand/charcoal tones, one clean phone mockup
- **Signature moment:** The "Concierge Pause" — chaos → brief pause → full itinerary appears
- **Key differentiators to feature:** halal badges, prayer times, زبده gists (honest pros/cons), during-trip assistance, Saudi expert personality
- **Monetization angle:** Lead with free, never lead with pricing. "Plan free, pay when you're ready."
- **Audiences:** Young Saudi pros (60%, speed+quality), Families (30%, peace of mind), GCC (10%, power user)
- **Tone:** Casual Saudi dialect, anti-hype, honest. "Your well-traveled friend" not "revolutionary AI"
- **Competitive play:** Never name competitors — contrast the *experience* (calm vs. panic, halal-first vs. generic, full-trip vs. booking-only)
- **Content pillars:** Friction stories, calm demos, real trips, during-trip wins, زبده honesty, behind-the-build

## 1) Brand Positioning Pillar

**One-sentence promise (anti-hype):**
"Your trip, thought through — not thrown together."

**What الزبدة is:** AI concierge, calm planning partner, Saudi travel expert you trust — from first idea to last day of the trip
**What الزبدة is NOT:** generic booking engine, "AI that plans for you," another OTA with filters

**Core differentiator (from BRD):**
The only Arabic-first, AI-powered, halal-aware trip planning app with during-trip assistance and an opinionated Saudi travel expert personality.

**Proof themes (pick 2–3 per campaign):**
- **Thoughtful curation** — halal badges, family-friendly picks, real local gems (not SEO tourist traps)
- **Day-by-day intelligence** — smart itineraries that respect geography, prayer times, and your pace
- **Personalization without interrogation** — understands your intent from minimal input (9 smart questions → full trip)
- **Calm UX** — no popups, no upsells, no "5 people are looking at this hotel right now"
- **Trust infrastructure** — transparent recommendations with زبده gists (honest pros/cons), not black-box rankings
- **With you the whole trip** — not just planning, but during-trip guidance: today's schedule, nearby halal, live AI help
- **Saudi travel expert personality** — opinionated, honest, culturally aware — like having a well-traveled Saudi friend

## 2) The "3-Step Humanized Loop" (Message Formula)

Use this exact pattern in every ad:

### A) Acknowledge the friction (real traveler moment)
- "When you've got 37 tabs open and still no plan…"
- "When everyone has an opinion but nobody has an itinerary…"
- "When you want halal options without having to research every restaurant yourself…"
- "When the trip is in 10 days and all you have is a flight…"
- "When you're planning for the family but also want something for yourself…"
- "When you're already there and don't know where to eat halal nearby…"
- "When the kids are hungry and you need a halal restaurant in 5 minutes…"
- "When you're abroad and can't figure out prayer times for this city…"

### B) Invite, don't solve (concierge language)
- "Tell us where. We'll think it through."
- "Bring the destination. We'll build the days."
- "You set the vibe. We'll handle the logistics."
- "Share what matters to you. We'll find what fits."
- "Pick your vibe. We'll find your destination."
- "Already there? We've got your day covered."

### C) Demonstrate unity + outcome (we language + tangible result)
- "We turn a destination into a trip that actually works."
- "We make every day make sense — not just every hotel."
- "We keep the plan yours, not ours."
- "We give you a trip you'd plan if you had 40 hours to research."
- "We stay with you — from planning to your last day."
- "We tell you what's great and what's not. Honestly."

**Rule:** Don't claim magic. Show a real trip taking shape — and a real trip being lived.

## 4) Creative System: Minimalist Editorial

### Layout rules (print, social, landing, OOH):
- 70% negative space ("breathing room before the trip starts")
- 1 short headline + 1 short subline + 1 CTA
- No feature lists in the ad itself (that's for the landing page)

### Visual cues:
- Warm off-white / sand tones + deep charcoal or navy type
- Typography: Playfair Display headlines, Readex Pro body (matches app)
- One "human" element only: a phone showing a clean itinerary, a hand holding a boarding pass, a map with a single pin, a family at a table, a solo traveler looking at a view
- Optional: split-screen — left side = tab chaos / right side = clean الزبدة itinerary

### Anti-ad twist (optional but viral):
- Show the "trip planning hell" (37 tabs, conflicting reviews, spreadsheet itinerary) vs. الزبدة's calm
- Parody the pushy booking site ("ONLY 1 ROOM LEFT!" "BOOK NOW OR REGRET FOREVER!") then cut to quiet, structured plan

## 5) The "Concierge Pause" Device (Signature Moment)

Every video version needs a 1–2 second pause where the plan comes together.

**Beat:** user shares a destination → brief pause → a structured day-by-day itinerary appears → exhale → user smiles / nods

This is الزبدة's fingerprint. The moment where "I have no plan" becomes "I have a trip."

### B) 12–15s Video Script (zbdh-style)

**0–2s (Friction):**
Close-up: someone scrolling through tabs — TripAdvisor, Google Maps, a WhatsApp group arguing about restaurants, a half-filled spreadsheet.
On-screen text: "Planning a trip shouldn't feel like a second job."

**2–5s (Anti-ad gag, optional):**
Pop-ups flood the screen: "ONLY 2 ROOMS LEFT!" "BOOK NOW!" "DEAL EXPIRES IN 3:00!" "TOP 50 THINGS TO DO IN—"
User closes laptop. Sighs.

**5–7s (Cut to calm):**
Phone screen. Clean wizard UI. Card-based questions.
User taps: family → culture & food → 5 days → moderate budget
On-screen text: "Tell us your vibe."

**7–11s (Concierge Pause):**
Brief pause. Then: a full day-by-day itinerary appears — hotels with halal badges, restaurants with زبده gists, activities organized by morning/afternoon/evening, prayer times woven in.
On-screen text: "We'll build the days."

**11–15s (Unity + CTA):**
User scrolls through the plan. Smiles.
On-screen: "Your trip, thought through."
Logo: **الزبدة** + "Plan your next trip — free."

## 7) Target Audience Messaging (from BRD)

### Primary: Young Saudi Professionals (60% of users)
- **Profile:** Age 25–40, SAR 15k–40k/month, 2–4 trips/year
- **Pain points:** Language barriers abroad, halal uncertainty, fragmented planning tools
- **Ad hook:** Speed + quality — "Full trip in 5 minutes, not 5 hours"
- **Key features to show:** Wizard flow, destination matching, زبده gists
- **Platform:** Instagram, TikTok, X (Twitter)
- **Tone:** Casual Saudi dialect, tech-savvy, peer-to-peer

### Secondary: Saudi Families (30% of users)
- **Profile:** Age 30–50, SAR 20k–60k/month, 1–2 trips/year
- **Pain points:** Planning for kids + adults, halal food abroad, safety concerns
- **Ad hook:** Peace of mind — "A trip that works for the whole family"
- **Key features to show:** Halal badges, prayer times, nearby discovery, pre-flight checklist
- **Platform:** Instagram, WhatsApp (sharing), YouTube
- **Tone:** Warm, reassuring, family-oriented

### Tertiary: GCC Expats & Nationals (10% of users)
- **Profile:** UAE/Kuwait/Bahrain residents, 3–5 trips/year
- **Pain points:** Need multi-language support, frequent travel
- **Ad hook:** Power user — "Unlimited trips, SAR 99/year"
- **Key features to show:** Annual plan value, trip history, Arabic-first UI
- **Platform:** Instagram, LinkedIn
- **Tone:** Sophisticated, value-conscious

## 8) Content Pillars (Weekly Ad Engine)

Rotate these so the feed stays consistent and "anti-hype":

1. **Friction Stories** — relatable trip-planning nightmares (the spreadsheet that got too long, the WhatsApp group that couldn't agree, the Booking.com panic)
2. **Behind the Build** — humanize the team, show values (why halal matters, why we don't do fake urgency, how the AI actually works)
3. **Calm Demos** — no fast cuts; one thoughtful workflow from "destination" to "full itinerary" (screen recordings, real trips)
4. **Real Trips** — showcase actual itineraries generated (with permission), different traveler types: family, solo, couple, group
5. **Anti-Clutter** — parody the noise of travel booking sites, then show الزبدة's calm
6. **Concierge Moments** — short clips of specific smart touches (e.g., "It suggested a halal breakfast spot 5 minutes from our hotel" / "It knew the kids needed a break after the museum" / "It showed me prayer times for every day")
7. **During-Trip Wins** — show the app being useful *while traveling* — nearby halal, today's schedule, live chat help (differentiator vs. all competitors)
8. **زبده Honesty** — show the gist system: honest 2-sentence takes that include downsides ("Great location but noisy at night" / "Amazing food, terrible service"). Build trust through transparency.

## 9) The "Value vs Motion" Test (Quality Gate)

Before publishing, ask:
- Does this ad show a real trip outcome or just vibes?
- Can someone repeat the message in 5 words? (e.g., "AI plans your trip properly")
- Does it feel calmer than the travel ads it sits next to?
- Would a Saudi traveler forward this to their group chat?
- Does it show something no competitor can claim? (halal-first, during-trip, Saudi personality)

If not: reduce elements, shorten copy, add whitespace, show one clear itinerary moment.

## 11) Audience-Specific Angles

### Families
- Friction: "Planning for kids + adults + grandparents = impossible"
- Promise: "One itinerary that works for everyone"
- Proof: halal restaurants, kid-friendly timing, prayer times built in, not too many stops per day
- During-trip angle: "Nearby halal when the kids are hungry. Prayer times when you need them."

### Solo Travelers
- Friction: "I don't want a tour group. I want a plan that feels like mine."
- Promise: "Your pace. Your interests. Structured, not rigid."
- Proof: budget awareness, walkable routes, local spots, honest gist reviews
- During-trip angle: "Ask the AI anything — it knows where you are and what's nearby."

### Couples
- Friction: "Every 'romantic getaway' list is the same"
- Promise: "A trip that actually fits what you both want"
- Proof: mix of adventure + rest, dining curation, surprise elements
- During-trip angle: "A concierge that suggests the perfect dinner spot while you're out exploring."

### Groups / Friends
- Friction: "The group chat has been arguing about the plan for 3 weeks"
- Promise: "One link. One plan. Everyone's happy."
- Proof: balanced schedule, variety, shared itinerary link (Annual plan feature)
- During-trip angle: "Today's plan is ready — no morning debates about what to do."

## 13) Tone of Voice Guide

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "We'll figure it out together" | "Our AI does it all for you" |
| "Smart planning" | "Revolutionary AI technology" |
| "Your trip, your way" | "The future of travel" |
| "Built for how you actually travel" | "Powered by cutting-edge algorithms" |
| Use casual Saudi Arabic naturally | Force formal Arabic (MSA) in ads |
| Show the itinerary result | Show the tech stack |
| Acknowledge the mess | Pretend planning is easy |
| "Here's what's great and what's not" | "5-star rated! Best hotel ever!" |
| "Plan free, pay when you're ready" | "Subscribe now for premium features" |
| "With you before and during the trip" | "End-to-end travel platform" |

**Voice = calm, competent concierge.** Think: the friend who's really good at planning trips, not a robot that replaced a travel agent. Opinionated but honest — like a well-traveled Saudi friend who tells you "skip the Grand Bazaar, go to the Egyptian Bazaar instead."
